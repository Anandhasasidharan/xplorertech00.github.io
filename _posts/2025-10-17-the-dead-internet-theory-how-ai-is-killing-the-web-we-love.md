<h2 class="wp-block-heading">The Two Faces of a Dying Internet</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I see the Dead Internet Theory playing out in two intertwined ways:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list {"ordered":true} -->
<ol class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>AI-Generated Content Flooding the Web</strong>: Right now, around 50% of online content is churned out by AI. Think endless articles, videos, and posts that look real but feel… off. It's efficient for creators and companies, but it drowns out genuine voices.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>AI Feeding on Fake Sources and Synthetic Data</strong>: AI systems are training on their own garbage—fake sources, looped synthetic data, and low-quality scraps. This creates a feedback loop of nonsense, where models spit out increasingly detached "reality."</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ol>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>These aren't isolated issues; they're how the internet operates today and how it's projected to evolve. We're all funneled into a handful of mega-platforms that amplify this cycle.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>And don't get me started on work tools like Teams and management software—they're just more algorithms dictating our days.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>As humans, we crave connection, belonging to our "tribe." But these platforms? They're weaponizing that instinct. Their designs aren't accidents; they're engineered for manipulation. Enter <strong>enshittification</strong>, a term coined by sci-fi author and tech critic Cory Doctorow to describe the inevitable decay of digital platforms. Here's how it works: Platforms start by delighting users with free, high-quality services—often subsidized at a loss—to hook us in. Once they're dominant, they squeeze users (think ads, data harvesting) to lure advertisers and businesses. Finally, they betray those businesses too, chasing investor dollars until everything turns to slop—endless ads, throttled features, and algorithmic sludge that prioritizes profit over people.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>This isn't random; it's the business model. Doctorow's theory explains why our favorite sites feel so hollow now—it's the endgame of platform capitalism colonizing the web. And it supercharges the Dead Internet: enshittified platforms lean harder on AI bots for "engagement" and synthetic content to cut costs, trapping us in a loop of manipulation and mediocrity.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>I know it's tough to quit—socially, professionally, emotionally. But ask yourself: are you interacting with real people, or just bots echoing your biases?</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">Bots Are Running the Show</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>For the first time in a decade, automated traffic has overtaken humans. In 2024, bots accounted for <strong>51% of all web traffic</strong>. That's not hyperbole—it's data. These digital zombies aren't just scraping sites; they're generating the content we consume, turning the internet into a ghost town of fake engagement.</p>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">The Slop of Synthetic Data: A Ticking Time Bomb</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Enter synthetic data—the go-to fix for AI companies like Microsoft, Google, and Meta when real, high-quality human data runs dry. It's artificially generated to train models, but it's a double-edged sword. Here's why it's problematic:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list -->
<ul class="wp-block-list"><!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Model Collapse</strong>: Errors pile up over generations, leading to AI outputs that are gibberish.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Bias Amplification</strong>: It bakes in flaws from the original models, spitting out unfair or toxic results.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Lack of Realism</strong>: Synthetic stuff misses the messy diversity of human creativity, making AI brittle and unpredictable.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Ethical Nightmares</strong>: What happens when AI drifts so far from human norms? Plus, no one's agreed on rules for creating it safely.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item -->

<!-- wp:list-item -->
<li><strong>Technical Hurdles</strong>: Scaling it is a nightmare, and if it mimics protected content, hello lawsuits and privacy scandals.</li>
<!-- /wp:list-item --></ul>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>In short, we're poisoning the well with our own shortcuts—exacerbated by enshittified platforms that prioritize cheap AI over quality.</p>
