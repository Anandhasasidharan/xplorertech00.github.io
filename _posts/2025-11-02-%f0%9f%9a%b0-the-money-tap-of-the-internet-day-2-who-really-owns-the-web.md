<h2 class="wp-block-heading">The Internet Isn’t Owned by 7 Giants—<strong>We</strong> Are the Internet</h2>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Your Wi-Fi router → ISP → Google/Amazon/Microsoft servers → <strong>your screen</strong>.<br>That tiny chain is the <em>real</em> internet. The “Big Tech” illusion? Marketing.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Yet dark patterns (endless scrolls, auto-play, notification bombs) keep us hooked.<br>And it all funnels through <strong>one gate</strong>: the browser.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:quote -->
<blockquote class="wp-block-quote"><!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>You don’t need a browser to access the internet</strong> — just a tool that speaks the right protocol (HTTP, FTP, SMTP, etc.).<br>But evolution (and manipulation) narrowed us down to browsers only.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph --></blockquote>
<!-- /wp:quote -->
